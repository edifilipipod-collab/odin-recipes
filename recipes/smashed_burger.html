A recipe for the world's best smashed burgers

Description

Smashed burgers are a fast, flavorful style of burger known for their thin, crispy-edged patties and juicy interior. Instead of shaping thick patties, the ground beef is pressed firmly onto a hot griddle or pan, creating maximum surface contact for a deep brown crust. This “smash” technique locks in flavor while giving the burger its signature caramelized, slightly crunchy exterior. Typically served on a soft bun with melted cheese and simple toppings, smashed burgers are all about bold beef flavor and texture rather than complexity.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Burger buns
  • Cheese slices
  • Salt and pepper
  • Butter (for toasting the buns)

Steps

  1. Heat a skillet or griddle over high heat until very hot.
  2. Divide the ground beef into loose balls (don’t overwork it).
  3. Place a beef ball on the hot surface, season with salt and pepper, and immediately smash it flat with a spatula. Cook for 2–3 minutes until a deep crust forms.
  4. Flip the patty, add a slice of cheese on top, and cook for another 1–2 minutes until done.
  5. Butter the burger buns and toast them on the pan until golden.
  6. Assemble the burger by placing the cheesy patty on the bottom bun, then topping with the other half of the bun.
