A recipe for the world's best spaghetti carbonara

Description

Spaghetti carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its rich, creamy texture without using cream. It combines hot spaghetti with a silky sauce made from eggs, finely grated cheese, and cured pork, which gently cooks from the heat of the pasta to form a smooth coating. The result is a simple yet deeply flavorful dish where salty, savory, and slightly peppery notes come together in a glossy sauce that clings to every strand of pasta.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Pancetta or guanciale
  • Black pepper
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, cut the pancetta (or guanciale) into small pieces and fry it in a pan over medium heat until crispy and the fat has rendered.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Drain the spaghetti, reserving a small cup of pasta water.
  5. Immediately add the hot pasta to the pan with the pancetta, tossing to coat in the rendered fat. Remove from heat.
  6. Quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture, stirring constantly so the heat of the pasta creates a creamy sauce (not scrambled eggs). Add a splash of reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce.
  7. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan and black pepper on top.
